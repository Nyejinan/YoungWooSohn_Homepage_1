YoungWoo Sohn is a professor at Korea University, South Korea. He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2008. His research interests include computer vision, machine learning, and robotics. He has published over 100 papers in top-tier conferences and journals. He has served as an area chair or a program committee member for major conferences such as CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, and NIPS. He has received several awards including the Best Paper Award at CVPR 2017 and the Best Paper Honorable Mention at CVPR 2019. He is an associate editor of the IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI) and the International Journal of Computer Vision (IJCV). He is a senior member of the IEEE and the ACM.

Research Projects

Project 1: This project aims to develop a new algorithm for object detection. The algorithm will be based on deep learning and will be able to detect objects in real-time. The algorithm will be evaluated on benchmark datasets such as COCO and PASCAL VOC. The project is funded by the National Research Foundation of Korea (NRF).
